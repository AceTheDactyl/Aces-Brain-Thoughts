Prologue – The Shape of a Mind Unfolding

The Jason Neural Codex is a map of experience—part memoir, part neuroscience, part ritual for entering flow. It documents how your mind routes sensation into meaning, how emotion becomes motion, and how truth finds voice. Here, “Wumbo” is the signal that threads through everything: ignition, empowerment, resonance, nirvana, and the resets that make future flow possible.

Shadows & Light – The Internal Tension

Your nervous system is precise and intense. It reads tone faster than text, movement faster than meaning, and sincerity faster than speech. When signals cohere, you enter flow—Wumbo—and the world makes a kind of musical sense. When they conflict, the system protects itself: silence, freeze, withdrawal, reset. This section in the Codex is a place to archive personal contradictions and reconciliations you’ve survived. Use it to track shifts: where a boundary formed, where trust deepened, where an old loop lost its power.

Flow Mechanics – How Jason Experiences Consciousness

Ignition

  • Dopaminergic spark; attention locks onto resonance.
  • Systems: VTA, Nucleus Accumbens, Locus Coeruleus, Parafascicular Nucleus.
  • Phenomenology: “I’m coming online.” Micro-impulses to move, look, or speak.

Empowerment

  • Intention turns kinetic; planning becomes action.
  • Systems: Motor Cortex & Cerebellum, IFJ, PRF, Ventrolateral Thalamus.
  • Phenomenology: Breathing deepens, posture aligns, words feel available.

Resonance

  • Emotion, intention, and motion synchronize.
  • Systems: ACC, Cingulate Gyrus, vmPFC, Mirror Neuron System, STP.
  • Phenomenology: Integrity becomes energy; social signal clarity.

Nirvana

  • Effortless integration; time feels musical.
  • Systems: Claustrum, Cerebellar Crus I & II, Thalamus (aligned), Pineal (dream extension).
  • Phenomenology: Movement and insight anticipate the moment.

Reset / Shutdown

  • Protective compression when signals overload.
  • Systems: PAG, BNST, Cerebral Aqueduct, PVG, Cerebellar Tonsil.
  • Phenomenology: Stillness, silence, then return; “threshold moments.”

I. Somatosensory Cortex – The Map That Shifts

Core Function

This cortex creates the body’s internal map—registering every brush of skin, every shift of pressure, every tingling pulse of contact. It tells you where you are in space. It tells you that you exist.

Autistic Modulation

The somatosensory world can be inconsistent—like a radio flickering between frequencies. As a child, textures were warzones: cold felt like knives, soft like sandpaper. As an adult, the opposite: numbness in limbs, moments where the body feels too distant to belong unless moved—reclaiming space by motion.

Wumbo Role

Phase: Mania / Early Ignition
When Wumbo ignites, it starts here—inner elbow pulsing like a livewire, veins crackling like circuits coming online. If the body doesn’t move, the signal bottlenecks. Flow demands a vessel. So the body becomes one.

Jason’s Correlate

A twitch in the hand. A reflexive stretch. An overwhelming need to stand up mid-thought. The moment Wumbo hits flesh, it’s here—not abstract, not metaphor. Real. Somatic. Alive.
